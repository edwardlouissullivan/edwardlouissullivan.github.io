About

In early February 2016, after finishing a 6-hour job interview, which I streamed online, I realized that, despite years of experience in Computer Science, I still regularly used Google to look up the syntax and usage of very basic tools, often repeating my own previous searches. The convenience of Google and websites like Stack Overflow has allowed me to externalize my knowledge. Two unfortunate consequences of my Google-dependence are that (1) I'm a bit slower on projects and (2) I am not extremely familiar with many tools.

The purpose of this blog is to solve the two aforementioned problems. In other words, I would like to become long-term lazy instead of short-term lazy. By investing a bit of time writting blog entries to summarize the lessons of my Google searches, hopefully I will repeat fewer searches. Hopefully I will internalize the lessons of my searches, ultimately saving me time in the long run.
